# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management process documentation. This README introduces the OctoAcme project management methodology and provides a comprehensive index of all core process documents.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. The framework is organized around five core phases: **Initiation** (validating business need and stakeholder alignment through a Project One-pager), **Planning** (breaking work into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs and defined acceptance criteria), **Execution** (managing day-to-day progress with daily standups and sprint reviews), **Release** (standardizing deployment processes to minimize risk), and **Retrospectives** (capturing learnings and driving continuous improvement). This lifecycle ensures that projects are grounded in measurable success metrics from the start and that delivery remains customer-focused throughout.

The organization operates with clearly defined roles that balance product strategy with delivery execution. **Product Managers** own the vision, prioritize the backlog, and validate solutions through data; **Project Managers** coordinate schedules, manage risks and dependencies, and maintain stakeholder alignment; and **Developers** implement features while collaborating on design, testing, and risk identification. This separation of concerns enables efficient decision-making while preserving psychological safety and shared ownership. Weekly syncs between PM and PdM, twice-weekly standups for delivery teams, and monthly stakeholder updates create multiple touchpoints for alignment and feedback.

Quality and risk management are embedded throughout the OctoAcme process rather than treated as afterthoughts. Execution includes unit testing, integration testing, and end-to-end smoke tests before release, along with security scanning in CI pipelines. A formal **Risk Register** is maintained across planning and execution phases, with risks tracked by ID, impact, likelihood, mitigation plan, and owner—reviewed weekly during syncs. Escalation pathways (team-level → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) ensure that blockers are resolved quickly, and a **Definition of Done** is established during planning to ensure consistent quality standards.

Communication and transparency anchor the entire framework. Status updates follow a standardized template (progress, next steps, risks/blockers, decisions needed), and a single source of truth—the project README or repository—keeps all stakeholders informed. Incident communication and blameless retrospectives formalize learning from failures, while action items from retrospectives are tracked with clear owners and due dates. This combination of structured workflows, defined personas, layered communication, and quality-first practices enables OctoAcme teams to deliver reliably while scaling institutional knowledge across the organization.
